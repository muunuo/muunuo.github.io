    Where to stay?

    First off, where should you stay? Well, I really recomand you stay in the South bank earea, why? First off, it’s right in the middle of the city and second, they have loads to do in the area, especially in the Parklands area. We stayed at the Brisbane Skytower, located just a 15 minuettes buss drive away from the Parklands.

    Day One

    So, on the first day of the trip, me and Jack went to the Queensland Museum, the perfect place for a history buff (like Jack), or even if you just like to learn. It’s right on the south bank, and has lots of exibits that are constently changing. Jack loved the dinosaur- and the wildlife exibits, while I really liked the guided tours of Say Our Names, where you get to hear personal insights and storys from the south sea ilanders. It was really toutching, but no spoilers so, moving on.

    Anyway, you want to hear the best part? It’s Free! :D For the most part, there was a few paid events you could opt into, we didn’t do this, because we didn’t relize you had to pre book a bunch of it, so the stuff we wanted to see was alredy fully booked, so don't make our mistake and cheak the website beforehand!

    After the museum we ended up walking over to the South Bank Parklands, a must visit. Theres just so mutch to do there, to the point where we actully had to come back a nother day. When we where there the first day tho, we went to the Brisbane sign for some pictures. Then the faris whele lokated just nearby for more pictures, Then we met up with some old friends of Jack and had a barbecue, (Aussies love their barbecues). Then we ended the day back at The South Bank Parklands taking the most beautifull and romantic walk along the bougavillie lined path going through the park.

    Day Two

    On the second day it was crazy hot, so we ended up spending most of the day at the Streets Beach over at the South Bank Parklands,(I said we would be back). It ended up being over 90 degrees fahrenheit, or about 33 degrees celsius as Jack would say. In other words, we where swetting like crazy for most of the day. We didn’t just stay at the beach the hole day tho, we did go to the rainforest walk nearby (after it cooled down a bit). We where also able to take a quick visit to the state library, whice I have to say, reminds me of a museum (something Jack absolutely loved). Like most of you know, Jack is orignally from Brisbane, so we where able to do most the things the library had to offer, if you’re not a Brisbane native, then you might have mutch more restrictions on what you can and can’t do, but it’s still worth visiting. After we meet up with Lilly, Jack’s big sister. She took us shopping at the Queen Street Mall nerby. After a bit of shopping we went to eat at Jimmy’s On The Mall.

    Day Three and Four

    Day 3 and 4 of the trip where spent with Jack’s family, now, they are a bit more privet (at least compered to me and Jack) so, to respect their privacy, I won’t go to in detail with what we did with them. So in broad strokes, on day 3 we actully checked out of our hotel, and took the hour drive down to the Gold Coast, where we stayed at the Mantra Sun City Surfers Paradise. We wanted to take a slow day, so me, Carol (Jack’s mom) and Tommy, (Lilly’s husband,) went shopping nearby, before hadding to the beach where we spent time relaxing. While Jack, Mark, (his dad) and Lilly played golf at a local course. We then meet up for dinner at the hotel. Day 4 was spent together, we visited som family in the area, before going snorkling with Gold Coast Dive Adventure, where Jack’s buddy works. Before heading back down the coast a bit to the Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary, where I got to meet a Koala, as well as many other Australian animals.
